Human-in-the-Loop AI Hiring Policy

Organizational Policy for AI-Assisted Employment Decisions

Template v1.0 | February 2025

1. Policy Statement

[ORGANIZATION NAME] is committed to using artificial intelligence in hiring responsibly and ethically. This policy establishes requirements for human oversight of all AI-assisted employment decisions to ensure fairness, legal compliance, and alignment with our values.

Effective Date: [DATE]

Policy Owner: [TITLE/DEPARTMENT]

Review Frequency: Annual (or upon significant regulatory changes)

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All AI-powered or automated tools used in the hiring process
  • Resume screening and parsing software
  • Candidate scoring and ranking algorithms
  • Video interview analysis tools
  • Chatbot-based candidate screening
  • Skills assessment platforms with algorithmic scoring
  • Any tool that uses machine learning to evaluate candidates

This policy covers all positions at [ORGANIZATION NAME], including:

  • Full-time employees
  • Part-time employees
  • Contract workers
  • Temporary positions
  • Internships

3. Definitions

Term Definition
AI Hiring Tool Any software or algorithm that analyzes, scores, ranks, or filters candidates during the hiring process
Human-in-the-Loop A system design where humans review, validate, or have authority to override AI-generated recommendations
Autonomous Decision A decision made by AI without human review or intervention
Borderline Candidate A candidate whose AI-generated score falls within a defined range requiring mandatory human review
Adverse Action Any decision to reject, not advance, or terminate consideration of a candidate

4. Human Oversight Requirements

4.1 Mandatory Human Review

Human review is REQUIRED for the following:

Scenario Review Requirement Reviewer
All adverse actions (rejections) 100% human review before rejection communication Recruiter or Hiring Manager
Borderline scores (60-85%) Mandatory human evaluation Recruiter
Candidates requesting human review Must be provided upon request Hiring Manager
Accommodation requests Human review of entire application HR + Hiring Manager
Final hiring decisions Human approval required Hiring Manager

4.2 Score Thresholds

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ AI SCORING DECISION FLOW │ └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘ Candidate Score Action Human Involvement ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── Score ≥ 85% → Auto-Advance Logged, human may review (High Confidence) Optional spot-check 60% ≤ Score < 85% → HUMAN REVIEW QUEUE MANDATORY human decision (Borderline) Document reasoning Score < 60% → Low Priority Queue Human may review (Below Threshold) Never auto-deleted

PROHIBITED: No candidate may be automatically rejected without human review. Low scores result in deprioritization, not automatic rejection.

4.3 Human Override Authority

Designated reviewers have full authority to:

  • Override any AI recommendation (advance or reject)
  • Request additional information or assessment
  • Flag AI decisions for audit review
  • Escalate concerns about AI performance

All overrides must be documented with reasoning in the applicant tracking system.

5. Documentation Requirements

5.1 Decision Logging

All AI-assisted decisions must include:

  • Timestamp of AI recommendation
  • Score/ranking and factors considered
  • Name of human reviewer (if reviewed)
  • Human decision (concur/override) and reasoning
  • Final disposition of candidate

5.2 Audit Trail Retention

Record Type Retention Period
AI decision logs Minimum 4 years
Human review documentation Minimum 4 years
Override records Minimum 4 years
Bias audit results Minimum 4 years (NYC: publicly accessible)

5.3 Explainability Requirements

AI tools must provide sufficient information for human reviewers to understand:

  • Which factors contributed to the score
  • The relative weight of each factor
  • Any red flags or concerns identified
  • How the candidate compares to requirements

6. Candidate Rights & Notifications

6.1 Pre-Application Notice

Candidates must be notified at least 10 business days before AI tools are used to evaluate their application. Notice must include:

  • Description of AI tools used
  • Types of data collected and analyzed
  • How to request human review
  • How to request an alternative process

6.2 Alternative Process

Upon request, candidates may:

  • Opt out of AI-assisted screening
  • Request full human review of their application
  • Request accommodation for disability-related needs

6.3 Post-Decision Rights

Candidates who are not selected may request:

  • General explanation of factors considered
  • Confirmation of human review
  • Information about how to reapply

7. Training Requirements

All employees involved in AI-assisted hiring must complete training on:

Topic Audience Frequency
This Human-in-the-Loop Policy All recruiters, hiring managers Initial + Annual
AI tool operation and limitations All AI tool users Initial + As updated
Recognizing and escalating AI bias All recruiters, hiring managers Annual
Documentation requirements All recruiters Initial + Annual
Candidate rights and accommodations All recruiters, hiring managers, HR Annual

8. Monitoring & Compliance

8.1 Regular Audits

  • Monthly: Review human override rates and patterns
  • Quarterly: Spot-check documentation compliance
  • Annually: Full bias audit by independent auditor (NYC LL144)

8.2 Key Metrics Tracked

  • Percentage of decisions reviewed by humans
  • Human override rate
  • AI-human agreement rate
  • Time from AI recommendation to human decision
  • Adverse impact ratios by protected class

8.3 Escalation Process

Concerns about AI tool performance or potential bias should be escalated to:

  1. [Immediate Supervisor]
  2. [HR Business Partner]
  3. [Chief People Officer/General Counsel]

9. Policy Violations

Violations of this policy may result in:

  • Verbal or written warning
  • Mandatory retraining
  • Removal from hiring responsibilities
  • Disciplinary action up to and including termination

Critical violations (e.g., automatic rejection without human review) must be reported to Legal within 24 hours.
